# Material Roles

Classification tells you what something is about.
Material role tells you what it is doing in the archive.

Without that distinction, drafts, references, exports, and primary work all start pretending to be equally important. They are not.

## Core roles

### source
Original authored or primary material.

### process
Work-in-progress material showing development.

### reference
Supporting or external material.

### output
Finalised or exported artefacts.

### residue
Low-value leftovers, noise, or artefacts.

### duplicate-candidate
Items suspected to be duplicates or unstable versions.

## Rules

- each item may have one material role
- roles do not replace anchors
- roles influence organisation decisions and note depth
- source and process usually deserve more attention than output or residue

## Short rule

Anchor says where it lives.
Role says why it matters.
