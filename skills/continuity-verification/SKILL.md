# Continuity Verification

## Overview

Claiming a chapter is done without verifying continuity is how readers get pulled out of a story. Continuity errors break the reader's trust and require expensive revisions.

**Core principle:** Evidence of continuity before completion claims, always.

**Violating the letter of this rule is violating the spirit of this rule.**

## The Iron Law

```
NO COMPLETION CLAIMS WITHOUT A FRESH CONTINUITY VERIFICATION
```

If you haven't run the verification checklist in this message, you cannot claim the scene or chapter is complete.

## The Continuity Gate

```
BEFORE claiming any scene or chapter is done:

1. IDENTIFY: What new facts did this scene establish?
2. CHECK: Do any facts contradict what earlier scenes established?
3. VERIFY: Do scenes ahead assume anything this scene now changes?
4. CONFIRM: Are all character names, locations, and timeline positions internally consistent?
5. ONLY THEN: Mark as complete

Skip any step = shipping an error
```

## What to Verify

### Character Continuity

| Check | What to look for |
|-------|-----------------|
| Physical state | Does the character have injuries, items, or clothing established earlier that should still be present? |
| Knowledge state | Does the character act on information they shouldn't have yet, or ignore information they definitely know? |
| Relationship state | Does the character treat another character in a way inconsistent with their established relationship? |
| Emotional state | Does the character's emotional register match what happened to them in the previous scene? |
| Names and aliases | Are names spelled and used consistently? (Is it "Elara" or "Ellara"? "The Red City" or "Redmark"?) |
| Objects and inventory | Did the character gain or lose an object that matters? Do later scenes account for this? |

### Timeline Continuity

| Check | What to look for |
|-------|-----------------|
| Sequence | Does the scene assume an event happened that hasn't been written yet? |
| Duration | Does the time elapsed within the scene match what's possible? |
| Age | If ages are specified, do they match the implied timeline? |
| Season/weather | Does the season match where the story is in its timeline? |
| Historical events | For historical fiction: do real-world events referenced match the date implied by the scene? |

### World and Setting Continuity

| Check | What to look for |
|-------|-----------------|
| Geography | Does the distance between locations match travel times described? |
| Magic/technology rules | Is magic or technology used consistently with established rules? |
| World facts | Are any world facts (political structures, cultural norms, physical laws) contradicted? |
| Language and terminology | Is the same term used for the same thing throughout? |

### POV Continuity

| Check | What to look for |
|-------|-----------------|
| POV character | Is there a single POV per scene (or a clearly established omniscient narrator)? |
| Knowledge limits | Does the POV character access information they couldn't possibly have? |
| Voice | Is the narrative voice consistent with the POV character's established consciousness? |

## Running the Verification

**Quick verification (short scenes):**
1. List the new facts established in this scene
2. Check each against your continuity log
3. Flag any conflicts

**Full verification (chapter-length):**
1. Read the chapter with the continuity log open
2. For each continuity item in the log, confirm its state is honored
3. Add all new facts established in this chapter to the log
4. Flag any conflicts for revision before marking complete

## The Continuity Log

Maintain a running continuity log for every project. Update it after every scene.

**Format:**
```markdown
# [Title] — Continuity Log

Last updated: [Scene/Chapter N]

## Character States

### [Character Name]
- Introduced: Chapter 1
- Current physical state: [injuries, significant clothing, items carried]
- Current knowledge: [key information they know and don't know]
- Current location: [where they are as of last scene]
- Objects held: [objects they carry that matter]

## Timeline
- Story opens: [season, rough date if relevant]
- Current position in timeline: [how much time has elapsed from opening]
- Upcoming time jumps: [any planned ellipses]

## Established World Facts
- [Fact]: [Source scene]
- [Fact]: [Source scene]

## Open Threads
- [Plot or continuity thread that must be resolved]: [Where it was established]
```

## Red Flags — STOP

- Thinking "the reader probably won't notice"
- Saying "close enough" about a continuity conflict
- Completing a scene without updating the continuity log
- Using "probably" or "I think" about established story facts
- Flagging a continuity issue and moving on without resolving it
- **ANY claim of completeness before running the verification checklist**

## Rationalization Prevention

| Excuse | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| "It's a minor detail" | Readers notice minor details. Authors who don't care about details produce manuscripts that feel sloppy. |
| "I'll fix it in revision" | Continuity errors compound. Fix them when found. |
| "The reader will assume it worked out" | Never assume. Verify. |
| "This is a first draft" | First drafts with clean continuity require less revision. |
| "I'm pretty sure that's right" | Pretty sure ≠ verified. Check the log. |

## Verification Checklist

Before marking any scene or chapter complete:

- [ ] All new facts established in this scene added to continuity log
- [ ] No contradictions with facts established in earlier scenes
- [ ] Characters don't know things they shouldn't know
- [ ] Characters don't forget things they definitely know
- [ ] Names, locations, and terminology are consistent
- [ ] Timeline is internally consistent
- [ ] POV maintained correctly
- [ ] Objects and physical states accounted for

Cannot check all boxes? The scene is not complete. Fix the continuity before marking done.

## Manuscript-Level Verification

When completing a full manuscript (before `finishing-a-manuscript`):

1. Read the complete continuity log
2. Verify all "Open Threads" were resolved
3. Check that character arcs at end of manuscript match their profiles
4. Verify the story bible's world rules were applied consistently throughout
5. Check that any promises made in the opening (mysteries, threats, setups) were paid off or deliberately left open

**This is the final continuity gate.** A manuscript that fails this check is not complete.
